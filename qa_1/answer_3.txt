Question (from finals page — Q3):
What does TF-IDF stand for? Explain in plain language why the word "the" scores almost zero in TF-IDF, while the word "photosynthesis" would score high.

Expected: Think about: what makes a word useful for identifying a specific document among many documents?

Answer (model):

TF-IDF = Term Frequency × Inverse Document Frequency.
- Term Frequency (TF): how often a word appears in a document.
- Inverse Document Frequency (IDF): a measure that downweights words that appear in many documents.

Plain explanation:
A word gets a high TF-IDF score when it appears frequently in one document but rarely across the whole collection — meaning it helps identify that document. "The" appears in almost every document, so its IDF is near zero and TF-IDF is near zero; "photosynthesis" is rare across documents but may appear several times in a biology article, so it gets a high TF-IDF score.

One-sentence summary:
TF-IDF highlights terms that are both frequent in a single document and rare across documents, making them useful identifiers.
