Question (from finals page — Q7):
What problem does RAG solve that a plain LLM (without RAG) cannot? Give a concrete example of when you would choose RAG over just prompting the LLM directly. Think about: knowledge cutoffs, private data, hallucinations, up-to-date information.

Expected: short explanation of RAG's role (retrieval of grounded facts), and a concrete example (private docs, up-to-date knowledge, reducing hallucination).

Answer:

RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) augments an LLM with a retriever that fetches relevant documents from an external knowledge store and conditions the model on those documents when generating answers. This solves three common LLM limitations: knowledge cutoffs (you can retrieve recent documents), private or proprietary data (you can search internal documents instead of exposing them to the base model), and hallucinations (the model can cite or base its output on retrieved facts).

Concrete example:
Customer support agent answering a user's account question: instead of asking the LLM to guess policy, the system retrieves the customer's account record and the current policy doc and provides those passages to the model so it can produce a precise, up-to-date, and verifiable reply. Without RAG, the LLM might hallucinate policy details or lack access to private account data.

One-sentence summary:
RAG combines retrieval and generation so the model can produce grounded, up-to-date, and private-data-aware answers that reduce hallucinations compared to a plain LLM.
